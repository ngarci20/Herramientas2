Hearts Beat Loud

What is it about?

Half a dozen local bands and musicians will perform at the Shack (St. Vincent Street, 42, New Jersey) this weekend, from 4 until 8 p.m. daily, to benefit each night's venue. On Friday, Jake Bugg will present his latest album, with an exclusive performance of its first single, All I Need. Indie band Cigarettes After Sex will follow. On Saturday, Lana del Rey will perform; a quick trip along classic Americana, from the 50's to the 70's and beyond. Death Cab for Cutie will come next as the band reunites for a special occassion. And on Sunday, we go back to the 80's with this year's breakout star Timecop1983. On their behalves, The XX will have the honor to close up the Festival.

A complete lineup and schedule is here.

The Hearts Beat Loud Festival is the first (and hopefully last) quarantine music festival. The goal of this event is to raise money for one of our favorite music venues, who is hurting right now due to COVID-19. We’re doing this through a three-day music festival over Instagram live (@heartsbeatloud) featuring some amazing and amazingly generous local acts.

Supporting local bands since 2006

In a sense, Hearts Beat Loud festival's roots can be tied back to the early 00's. In 2001, local artist who wanted to make ther way through the music industry, yet were more or less unknown, would travel to musical retreats known as camp meetings to camp out and bond over their love for music with other artists. The Shack was nearby one of these retreats, and so it was only a matter of time before they all reunited to grab a bear or two and start showing each other what they got.

But it wasn't until 2006 that we saw a diverse crowd gather around for a festival, mostly focused on indie music, inside the walls of this club. In 2008, over 1,500 people gathered together for the then called Beaters Festival (which had to be changed in early 2010 for obvious reasons), where artists like The Killers and The Perishers came to perform. It was America's first ever indie festival. And the rest is History.

Some of the bands that once performed for us are now vastly known on a world level:

  • Oasis
  • The Smiths
  • Muse
  • Bloc Party
  • Imagine Dragons
